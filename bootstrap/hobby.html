🦋 Origami Butterfly

🦋 Origami Butterfly Step-by-Step Instructions:

You‚Äôll Need:

One square sheet of paper (any size, but 15x15 cm is good for beginners)

✂ Steps:

1. Start with the colored side up (if using colored paper).

  • Fold the paper in half diagonally to make a triangle.
  • Unfold.
  • Now fold it along the other diagonal. Unfold again.

    • -You should now have an "X" crease across the square.


    2. Flip the paper over.

  • Fold the paper in half vertically, then unfold.
  • Fold it in half horizontally, then unfold.

    • 5. Fold the bottom corner up.

    • 6. Fold the model in half.

    • 7. Shape the butterfly.

    🕊 Paper Cranes

    🕊 Step-by-Step Instructions:

    You‚Äôll Need:

    One square sheet of paper (any size, but 15x15 cm is good for beginners)

    ✂ Steps:

    1. Start with the colored side up (if any).

  • Fold the paper in half diagonally to make a triangle. Unfold.
  • Fold along the other diagonal and unfold.

    • -You now have an "X" crease.


    2. Flip the paper over.

  • Fold the paper in half vertically, unfold.
  • Fold in half horizontally, unfold.

    • -You now have both diagonal and straight creases.


    3. Collapse into a square base.

  • Using the creases, bring all corners together to collapse the paper into a smaller square (diamond-shaped), with the open flaps pointing down.

    • 4. Create the kite shape (called the bird base).

  • Take the right lower flap and fold the bottom edge to the center crease. Do the same with the left flap
  • Fold the top triangle down (to make a crease), then unfold all three folds.
  • Lift the bottom flap up, and as you lift it, fold in the sides along the creases to form a tall diamond.
  • Flip the paper over and repeat the kite folds on the other side.

    • 5. Form the crane's neck and tail.

  • Fold the bottom edges of both sides into the center crease again (on both front and back).
  • You should have a long, narrow diamond.
  • Fold one of the side flaps upwards to form the neck.
  • Do the same on the other side to form the tail.

    • 6. Make the head.

  • On the neck side, fold the tip down to create a head/beak.

    • 7. Fold down the wings.

  • Gently pull down the top flaps (the wings) on each side and flatten.

    • 🎉 Done!

    You've made an origami paper crane! 🕊

    It‚Äôs a symbol of peace, hope, and healing. In Japanese tradition, folding 1,000 cranes (senbazuru) is said to grant a wish or bring good luck.

    🐉 Step-by-Step Instructions:

    You‚Äôll Need:

    One square sheet of paper (any size 15x15 cm works great)

    ✂ Steps:


    1. Start with the colored side up (if any).

    • -Now your paper has both diagonal and straight creases.


    3. Collapse into a square base (preliminary base).

    • - This forms the bird base.


    5. Form the body and tail.

    • -This gives you a long, narrow diamond shape.

  • Fold the opposite flap upward ‚Äì this will be the tail.

    • 6. Make the head.

  • On the neck flap, fold down the tip to create the dragon‚Äôs head or snout.

    • 7. Fold out the wings.

  • Gently pull down the top flaps on each side ‚Äî these become the dragon‚Äôs wings.
  • Adjust and flatten as needed to shape them.

    • 🎉 Done!

    You‚Äôve made a simple origami dragon!
